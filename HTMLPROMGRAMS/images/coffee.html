.......Diffrent Types Of Coffee.......

Black Coffee

It is rich in caffeine that may stimulate the metabolism and increases your energy level, suppressing hunger. Black coffee contains a chlorogenic acid that helps to slow down the production of glucose. Hence, if you drink a black coffee after a meal, it can help to slow down glucose.

Cinnamon Coffee

The sweet and woody taste of cinnamon makes it another spice that goes exceptionally well with chocolate. Although slightly sweet, the woody flavour is more prominent and has a subtle yet noticeable impact on every dish it is used in.

Chocolate Coffee

Chocolate coffee, also known as the 'Mocha', is a delightful combination of chocolate and coffee. A mocha is typically based on the combination of an espresso and hot milk with added chocolate flavours, either through cocoa powder or chocolate syrup, and additional sweetener for those with a big sweet-tooth.

Cappichino Coffee

A cappuccino is a beloved espresso-based hot coffee drink made with layering of espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam on top. The taste of cappuccinos can be described as creamy, smooth, and balanced. The combination of flavors and textures creates a delightful and comforting experience for many coffee enthusiasts.

Iced Coffee

Quite simply, an iced coffee is a cold version of your favourite coffee, but it's not simply a coffee that has been left to cool. Like a regular coffee, it's usually a combination of hot espresso and milk. However, ice is added too..

Expresso Coffee

Espresso is thicker and more intense than coffee because of the lower grounds to water ratio, the finer grind, and the pressurized brewing method. Regular coffee uses a coarser grind, more water and gravity to extract the final brew..
